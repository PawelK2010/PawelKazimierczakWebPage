European School Science Symposium

Rehabilitation of Wrist Injuries

Pawel Kazimierczak, Javier Tejedor Granado, Jean-Luc Fael Bernabe

This project presents a low-cost rehabilitation glove designed to help support wrist recovery exercises in a more accessible way.

The work was awarded the First Innovation Award at the European School Science Symposium.

Motivation

Hand and wrist injuries are common, and rehabilitation can be expensive or difficult to access often enough outside professional care. The project was developed to explore a cheaper, more practical solution that patients could also use at home.

Project Idea

The report describes a glove attached to an elastic band and an arm support system. By changing the position of the fabric on the upper arm, the resistance can be adjusted more easily according to rehabilitation needs.

The design aimed to stay affordable by using recycled or inexpensive materials while remaining durable enough for repeated use.

Testing Procedure

The prototype was tested over four weeks with three users. Progress was monitored by measuring how many kilograms could be moved with the wrist, comparing weekly results against the starting values.

Main Findings

  • All tracked results showed positive improvement during the testing period.
  • The strongest gains appeared early, especially during the first week of practice.
  • The report highlights a strong improvement in flexion and extension, with one participant improving by more than 40 percent from the initial measurement.

Conclusion

The project shows that a simple and low-cost rehabilitation tool can still offer useful support for wrist recovery. The idea combines accessibility, sustainability, and practical testing in a way that makes the prototype especially relevant for innovation-focused student research.
